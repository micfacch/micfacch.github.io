Chidea: Facilitating the Velocity of ideas with Twitter For City Leaders

A case study competition

Nov 2013 - Won case study competition through Startup Institute Chicago and ChicagoNext by designing a technology solution that increases transparency and comunication among board members. Please note this is not a real world application but a conceptual design.

Business leaders sitting on boards that meet infrequently have a need to collaborate in a way that is not demanding of their time or effort

Current communication protocols are too labor intensive, in noisy channels, or lacking transparency.

With Chidea, when Eric Lefkofsky a board member of ChicagoNext is hit with a spark of genius for something to do with ChicagoNext, he is able to effortlessly share that with other members of ChicagoNext and has the option of sharing it with the public as well.

A living history of the groups ideas, in-progress items, and completed projects..

Here, Eric is able to post about his new concept for ChicagoNext. The format creates a place for ideas to be created that is cheap. Cheap meaning, there is little social risk since there is not an expectation for a pixel perfect communication.

It is added to the feed under ideas.

The other members of the group get a notification of this posting.

They are able to build onto this idea or give constructive feedback.

A history is created and progress is tracked; adding accountability and transparency.

The concept is completed and a press event created.

The web interface allows users to see the entire timeline at once, and allows for the possibility of public feedback.

People are comfortable with this medium. The public has interest in knowing the inner-workings of its cities leaders.

ChicagoNEXT board members are interested!

Other tools are not tailored to the specific needs of city leaders to collaborate on the boards they sit on with convenience and transparency.

The better we can get leaders to collaborate the better we can create jobs.

Jobs that motivated and smart people need and want to fill.
